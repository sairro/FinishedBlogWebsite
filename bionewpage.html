Biography Section

January 9, 2026 • 6 min read • Ailexander Kylle Cusi

Childhood Years

1I was born in a small barangay where everyone knew each other. 2Growing up, I spent most of my days playing outside with my neighbors. 3Our street became our playground, filled with running, shouting, and endless laughter. 4I enjoyed climbing trees even though my mother always told me to be careful. 5Every afternoon, I looked forward to the sound of the sorbetero’s bell. 6I loved the simple joy of eating dirty ice cream under the hot sun. 7My father often brought home pasalubong, and it always made me excited. 8My mother taught me basic household chores while telling stories about her childhood. 9I always felt warmth in our home even if it was small. 10I remember listening to the radio with my grandparents after dinner. 11Weekend mornings meant going to the palengke with my parents. 12I liked watching the vendors arrange colorful fruits and vegetables. 13Whenever it rained, my siblings and I played outside and jumped in puddles. 14At night, I loved staring at the stars because my grandmother said each one had a story. 15I enjoyed helping my father water the plants in our tiny garden. 16Sometimes, I pretended to be a superhero while running around the house. 17I also loved joining the barangay fiestas every year, amazed by the colorful parols and street decorations. 18I remember helping my mother cook puto and bibingka for the neighbors. 19On Sundays, we went to the chapel, and I loved the sound of church bells. 20Those moments taught me kindness, gratitude, and the value of community.

Teenage Years

1My teenage years were filled with big changes and new challenges. 2I started to become more aware of my responsibilities. 3High school life felt exciting but stressful at the same time. 4I met new friends who understood my personality. 5We loved hanging out after class even if we didn’t have much money. 6I learned how important it was to balance fun and schoolwork. 7There were days when I felt pressured to get good grades. 8My parents always reminded me to stay focused and patient. 9I joined different school activities to explore my interests. 10Sometimes, I struggled with self-confidence. 11I experienced my first crush during those years, which made my heart race and my days brighter. 12I joined the school’s cultural dance club and performed during Independence Day celebrations. 13Sometimes, I stayed after class to tutor younger students in math and Filipino. 14I discovered that helping others gave me a sense of pride and accomplishment. 15My weekends were often spent at the basketball court, practicing with friends. 16I loved attending Simbang Gabi during Christmas with my family, feeling connected to my Filipino roots. 17I sometimes felt anxious about fitting in, but I learned to embrace my uniqueness. 18I experimented with hobbies like sketching and writing short stories. 19Teenage life taught me resilience, creativity, and self-awareness. 20By the end of high school, I felt more ready to face the challenges of the future.

College Years

1College life felt like entering a completely different world. 2I was excited but nervous on my first day. 3Everything looked bigger and more overwhelming than high school. 4I met classmates from various places in the Philippines. 5We bonded over group projects, late-night studying, and shared struggles. 6I learned to manage my time more wisely. 7There were days when I barely slept because of deadlines. 8I experienced moments when I doubted my abilities but pushed myself to keep going. 9I joined organizations that helped me grow socially and academically. 10College taught me that mistakes are part of learning. 11I discovered my passion for technology and coding through challenging assignments. 12I sometimes cooked simple meals in the dorm, like sinigang or adobo, and shared them with friends. 13I made lifelong friends who became like siblings, supporting me emotionally and academically. 14I learned to budget my allowance carefully, prioritizing essentials over wants. 15College allowed me to travel for competitions and events, experiencing places I had never visited before. 16I sometimes volunteered in outreach programs, helping local communities and teaching kids basic skills. 17I learned to handle stress through meditation, journaling, or listening to music. 18I became more confident in presenting my ideas in class. 19I appreciated the diversity around me, learning about traditions, languages, and cultures from different parts of the Philippines. 20By the end of college, I felt ready to face the challenges of the real world, carrying memories, friendships, and lessons that would stay with me forever.
