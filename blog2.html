In Praise of the Generalist

January 9, 2026 • 3 min read • Ailexander Kylle Cusi

Opening

Siguro you're wondering, why the weird and short title? Actually that sums up 20 years of my life living in this, no trees (almost, just give a few more years), polluted, poverty on top world. I'm a single entity living peacefully waking up in the morning with the same old coffee in my hand. A warrior with many skills, I possess strength beyond a single blade being poetic, but in reality? I'm just a Lost kid forged by many paths. I always think only about balance and the value of many skills, but never the mastery hence, The Generalist. I've always wanted to play with kids my age but they all seem to be better than me in their field and then there's me, the so called average, the so called "pwede na yan", it seems like the time where the lime light would be on my feet will never arrive.

The Details

I'm a champion, I have 3 championships on my name sa larong siguro kalahati sa mga kasama ko ngayon hindi nila alam, even tho I'm a Jack of All Trades, I seem to balance everything in my life, from playing games to studying and learning how to code properly because that's my chosen field. Okay wait I know you're wondering, what do I mean by meron akong 3 championships on my name? well, you're in for a story. Gaming has been one of the most significant parts of my life. What started as a simple hobby eventually became a platform for growth, discipline, and achievement. I dedicated just a bit few hours playing online, learning strategies, improving my skills, and understanding how to work effectively with a team. I became part of a PUBG Mobile Esports Team called GodPlay kung saan nakalaro, nakakampi, at nakalaban ko ang mga halimaw sa larangan na pinasok ko, I competed on the North America server. Representing my team and performing in tournaments was both challenging and rewarding. Becoming an esports tournament champion was not just about winning it was about patience, leadership, communication, and resilience. Oh? why did I bother to tell the story? because I believe that adaptation will always be on top against specialization. I adapted through countless pressures, criticism, hell even trashtalk hence, The Generalist.

Resolution

Looking back at these 20 years, being a jack of all trades has shaped me into someone who can adapt to any situation life throws my way. While others may have mastered single skills, I've built a foundation of versatility that allows me to balance gaming championships, coding studies, and everything in between. Sure oo, there were moments when I felt like I was always second best and to be honest, 9 times out of ten nafefeel ko, never quite reaching that spotlight I craved, but I've learned that my strength lies in my ability to navigate multiple worlds easily. Through countless pressures, criticism, and challenges, I've discovered that adaptation isn't just a survival skill it's a superpower that keeps me resilient in this damned world. These two decades have taught me that being "pwede na yan" in many things is actually more valuable than being perfect in just one, because life demands flexibility, not just expertise.
